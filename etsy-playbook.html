PROLOGUE — what you're holding

📋 What this is

This is a shooting script, the kind a Hollywood director hands to actors before a scene. It tells you EXACTLY three things, line by line:

  1. SAY — the exact words to speak (italics, in quotes). Each line has a 📋 Copy button so you can drop it into NoorCast's teleprompter.
  2. DO — the exact button to click in NoorCast or key to press on your keyboard.
  3. VISUAL — what your face / hands / camera should be doing (smile, eye contact, gesture).

Plus: a pre-flight checklist so you don't miss a setting, a storyboard so you can see the whole 60 seconds at a glance, director's tricks for energy and pacing, and a troubleshooting matrix for when things go wrong.

If you've never recorded a video before, read the script through twice silently first. Then read it out loud once. Then start the pre-flight. By the time you press REC you'll know your lines.
Use the floating teleprompter dock in the bottom-right of this page. It has a "📤 Send all dialogue to NoorCast Teleprompter" button — copies every line to your clipboard so you can paste them straight into NoorCast's built-in teleprompter (T to toggle).

ACT ONE — pick your script

🎭 The 3 scripts

Three flavours. Pick the one closest to your listing. Each is a complete 60-second script with SAY / DO / VISUAL for every beat. Click 📋 Copy on any line to copy that exact text. ▶ Read on any line plays it aloud (your browser's voice) so you hear the cadence.

Use case: showing a product you made (pottery, jewelry, art, kit, anything physical or digital). Etsy's most common video format.

Scene 1 — INT. STUDIO – DAY · OPENING SHOT ⏱ 0:00 → 0:03 HOOK
SAY (big energy, eye contact)
Three seconds — here's what I just made.
(Tone: punchy, like you're stopping someone in the street. Don't trail off.)
DO
Recording is already running. Don't touch anything. Hold your product up at chin height, centered in the safe zone. If your face-cam has the product physically with you, scene 👋 You (or scene 1) is fine — face fullscreen.
VISUAL
Big, real smile. Look directly into the camera lens (not the screen). Hold the product so it's clearly visible. Don't move your head — let the product do the moving.
CUT TO:
Scene 2 — CONTEXT · THE PROBLEM ⏱ 0:03 → 0:10 CONTEXT
SAY
Most people buy these and then don't know it can do this one weird thing.
(Replace "this one weird thing" with your actual hook — the surprising feature. Keep it under 12 words.)
DO
If your demo is on a screen: press 2 to switch to scene 💻 Code (full screen view). If your demo is physical: stay on face-cam scene. Press M right at the word "this" — drops a chapter marker called "Hook reveal."
VISUAL
Eyes go wide on the word "weird." Slight head tilt. If holding the product, lean it slightly forward toward the camera on "this."
CUT TO:
Scene 3 — THE DEMO · DO THE THING ⏱ 0:10 → 0:35 DEMO
SAY · BEAT 1 (0:10–0:18, slow down here)
Watch — I'll show you in one move.
DO · BEAT 1
Do the action. Press L if you need to point at something on screen — laser pointer. Press F at the climax of the action — freezes the canvas for 1 sec, gives the viewer time to absorb it.
VISUAL
Slow your hands by 30%. People always rush demos. If your hands are blurry, you went too fast.
SAY · BEAT 2 (0:18–0:28)
And here's how it works behind the scenes.
DO · BEAT 2
Switch to a multi-source scene if you have one — 5 is usually 🎬 Studio (split: face + screen + 2nd cam). Use 🔴 Laser to point at the part you're explaining.
VISUAL
Glance at the laser, then back to camera. Don't STARE at the screen. You're talking to a person, not narrating.
SAY · BEAT 3 (0:28–0:35)
It takes about 30 seconds, and you don't need anything I haven't put in the kit.
DO · BEAT 3
Press M right after the word "kit" — chapter marker "Reassurance." Stay on the multi-source scene.
VISUAL
Genuine, calm. This is the trust beat. Don't oversell. Look directly at the lens.
CUT TO:
Scene 4 — THE PAYOFF · SHOW THE RESULT ⏱ 0:35 → 0:50 PAYOFF
SAY
…and the result is this.
(Pause for 2 full seconds AFTER you say "this." This is THE most important silence in the whole video. Don't fill it.)
DO
Press 1 to switch back to face-cam fullscreen (👋 You). Hold up the finished product / show the result clearly. 2-second pause — count "one Mississippi, two Mississippi" silently in your head. Don't fill it with "uh."
VISUAL
Slow real smile. Hold the result up close to your face so both are in frame together. The 2-sec silence is when viewers DECIDE if they want it. Let them.
CUT TO:
Scene 5 — CALL TO ACTION · SEND THEM TO ETSY ⏱ 0:50 → 0:55 CTA
SAY
You can grab one in my Etsy shop — link is right below this video.
(Conversational. Like you're recommending a friend's place. NOT salesy.)
DO
Stay on face-cam fullscreen. Don't switch scenes — viewers need to see your eyes for the CTA. Let your watermark (the @yourhandle pill in the corner) speak for the brand.
VISUAL
Eye contact. Slight nod on "right below." A small confident nod, not a head bob. Don't smile too wide here — that reads as needy. Keep it warm, not desperate.
FADE TO:
Scene 6 — END FRAME · THE LOOP SEAM ⏱ 0:55 → 0:60 END FRAME
SAY
Thanks for watching.
(Soft. Almost a whisper. Then SILENCE for 3 full seconds.)
DO
Don't move. Don't blink fast. Hold a calm, neutral-warm expression for 3 seconds. This frame becomes your video cover AND the loop seam. Then press R to STOP.
VISUAL
Final frame composition: face top-third, watermark visible, no movement. This is the still that Etsy shows when the video isn't autoplaying. Make it a frame you'd be happy to see paused.

DIRECTOR'S CHAIR — secrets the pros use

🎬 Director's tricks

The 5-Take Rule (most important)

Never publish your first take. Plan for 5. Each take auto-saves to NoorCast's history. Use the header 🎬 Last take button to revisit.

TakeGoalWhat to fix
1Read the script literally.Find awkward sentences. Mark them.
2Fix the awkward bits.Less reading, more talking.
3Drop the script entirely.Talk to a friend, not a camera.
4Slow down 10%. Add 1 deliberate pause.Find the rhythm.
5Your keeper.Smile, ship.

The "look at the lens, not the screen" trick

Beginners stare at the preview. Your eyes look DOWN in the recording. Put a tiny sticker next to your camera lens — a smiley face works. Talk to that sticker. Your eye contact will jump from "creepy" to "magnetic" in one take.

The 3-second pause = trust

After your CTA, hold silence for 3 full seconds before STOP. Beginners feel this is awkward. Viewers feel it as confidence. The Etsy loop starts cleanly. Your loop seam disappears.

The teleprompter trick

NoorCast has a built-in teleprompter (toolbar → ⋯ More → 📜 Tele, or press T). Click 📤 Send all dialogue on each script tab to copy every line. Then in NoorCast, open the teleprompter, paste, set scroll speed to 30–40 wpm (your reading speed), and start recording. Look at the lens, not the teleprompter — let the words scroll in your peripheral vision.

The energy multiplier

Whatever energy you bring to the recording, the viewer gets HALF of it. If you feel "normal," the viewer feels "tired." If you feel "150%," the viewer feels "good." So always overdo it. Big smile. Big eyes. Big hand gestures. You'll watch the playback and think "yikes, too much" — and the viewer will think "natural and warm."

Audio is 60% of the perceived quality

Cheap mic + great camera = unprofessional. Cheap camera + great mic = professional. If you only invest in one upgrade, get a $30 lavalier mic. Sound quality buys trust faster than 4K resolution.

The chapter-mark technique

Press M at every interesting moment during recording. After STOP, in the take modal, click ✨ Auto-chapter from transcript — it ADDS more chapters from your speech (topic shifts, transition words). You end up with a perfectly-segmented video that has a built-in table of contents.

The "freeze on result" emphasis

Press F right when the result appears. The canvas freezes for 1 second — drives home that THIS is the moment. Especially powerful for unboxings, transformations, and reveals.

POST-PRODUCTION — after the take

🎁 After STOP

The instant you press STOP, NoorCast opens the Take modal — a fullscreen overlay with your video on the left and ALL post-recording tools on the right. Work down the right column in this order:

  1. Watch playback once. All the way through. Eyes off your script. Does it land? If no, click 🎬 Nouveau tuto and re-record.
  2. If take is over 60 seconds: click 📱 Cut to 90 s for Reels. Real in-browser re-encode (~real-time speed). Output is named <name>-reels.mp4.
  3. Click ✨ Auto-chapter from transcript. Adds smart chapter markers based on your speech — perfect for the SRT export and any platform that supports chapters.
  4. Read the MISSION COMPLETE card (Kids mode). Cool factor < 60? You probably need more energy / scenes / chapters. Re-record.
  5. Type a clean title in the filename input. Something like etsy-listing-coastal-pottery-2026.mp4.
  6. Rate the take (5-star widget). Helps you remember which take was the keeper across multiple sessions.
  7. Click 📥 Download (the green primary button). Saves the MP4 to your downloads folder.
  8. Click 💬 Captions (.srt). Save the subtitles. You'll paste these into the Etsy listing description for SEO.
  9. Click 🖼 Thumbnail (.png). Saves the first frame. Good for the Etsy listing's product photo array.
  10. Close the modal (X or ESC). NoorCast remembers — re-open via the header 🎬 Last take button if you need to re-download anything.
If you want to tweak BEFORE uploading: open the in-browser trim modal (✂️ Couper button) — gives you in/out scrubbers, scene-band visualization, and exports a re-encoded clip with the same quality.

Etsy upload checklist

  1. Open Etsy → your shop → the listing → Photos & Video section.
  2. Click + Add a video. Upload your MP4. Etsy compresses it (you can't avoid this — that's why we recorded at 8 Mbps).
  3. Use the captions text (from your SRT file) in the listing description. Etsy's search reads descriptions. Words you said in the video become discoverable.
  4. The listing title should match your video's first words. If you said "60 seconds to learn macramé," the title should be "Learn macramé in 60 seconds — full kit + video tutorial."
  5. Tag the listing with the words you actually said. Etsy's recommendation algorithm uses tag-to-search matching.
  6. Save. Preview the listing as a buyer. Watch your video AS A BUYER would. Does it stop your scroll?

DICTIONARY — what the words mean

📖 Glossary for beginners

SOURCE
One input layer on your canvas. A camera, a screen-share, a mic, an image. You can have many.
SCENE
A saved arrangement of sources. Click a scene tile in the right sidebar to switch instantly. Press 1–9 for the first nine.
CANVAS
The big rectangle in the middle. What gets recorded. Anything outside it is ignored.
SAFE ZONE
The green dashed box in 9:16 mode. The area where your viewer will actually SEE the video — Etsy / FB / TikTok UI covers the red areas.
CHAPTER MARKER
A timestamped label inside the video. Press M to drop one. Saved in the .vtt sidecar file. Useful for navigation in long takes.
WATERMARK
A small text/logo overlay in the corner of every recording. Set in Settings → Slogan. Becomes your "brand stamp."
WORKFLOW STEP
The 4 tabs above the toolbar (SETUP / TUNE / RECORD / SHARE). Auto-advances as you progress. Tells you what to focus on.
STATUS BAR
The single line above the REC button. Shows current state, source count, format, bitrate, your handle. Click any chunk to change that setting.
TAKE
One recording attempt. NoorCast saves all takes — you can re-open any of them via the header 🎬 Last take button.
BURN-IN CAPTIONS
Subtitles painted directly onto the video pixels (vs. a separate text track). Survives social-media compression. Mandatory for muted-autoplay platforms like Etsy and Reels.
HOOK
The first 3 seconds of your video. The single most important segment. If the hook fails, nothing else matters.
CTA
"Call To Action" — the line that tells the viewer what to do next ("link below", "tag me with"). Always near the end, never in the middle.
END FRAME / LOOP SEAM
The last frame of your video. On Etsy/Reels it becomes the cover AND the loop continuation point. Make it visually clean.
B-ROLL
Background visuals that play under your voiceover (e.g. screen recording while you talk to camera). NoorCast does this via multi-source scenes.
TELEPROMPTER
A scrolling script overlay you read from while looking at the camera. Press T in NoorCast to toggle.
