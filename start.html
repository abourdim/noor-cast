NoorCast

Record multi-camera tutorial videos from your browser. Perfect for teaching code with a robot.

Open NoorCast →

What you need

  • A laptop or desktop computer
  • Chrome or Edge browser (desktop only)
  • A webcam (built-in or USB)
  • A microphone (built-in or external)
  • Optional: phone(s) as extra cameras, micro:bit for sensors

Your first recording in 7 steps

1

Open NoorCast

Open index.html in Chrome or Edge. Or visit the hosted version at abourdim.github.io/noorcast.

2

Allow permissions

Chrome asks for camera and microphone access. Click Allow. You'll see your face in the Preview box.

3

Add your sources

Screen: click + Add under Screen → pick a window or tab.
Camera: select from dropdown → + Add.
Phone camera: install Iriun or DroidCam → phone appears in dropdown.
Drag and resize each source on the black canvas.

4

Pick a template

Click "Pick a template" at the bottom. Choose one:

📚

Full Lesson

Intro → Theory → Demo → Exercise → Recap

🤖

Robot Demo

Face → Code → Robot → Sensors → Recap

🔧

Fix-it

Bug → Analyze → Fix → Test → Done!

🤖

Robot Tuto

Intro → Code → Robot → Sensors → Recap

The template creates a step strip — click each step during recording to switch scenes.

5

Hit REC

Click the big red ● REC button. A 3-2-1 countdown plays, then you're recording!

6

Record your tutorial

Click the template steps to switch scenes. Use shortcuts while recording:

M chapter marker · L laser pointer · F freeze · D draw · 1-6 switch scenes

7

Stop and download

Click Stop. Your video downloads as .webm (or .mp4) with a .vtt chapter file. Use the Trim tool to cut the start/end, or Silence Trim to auto-remove pauses.

Want to add a phone camera?

The easiest way: install Iriun Webcam on your phone + computer. It's free, supports portrait mode, and works on all platforms.

  1. Phone: App Store · Play Store
  2. Computer: iriun.com
  3. Same Wi-Fi → auto-connects
  4. In NoorCast: + Camera → "Iriun Webcam" → + Add

More options: DroidCam, Camo, Continuity Camera →

Want to connect your micro:bit?

Click 📡 Connect micro:bit in the left sidebar. Pair via Bluetooth. Your micro:bit becomes a remote control:

  • Button A = zoom in
  • Button B = drop chapter marker
  • Tilt = aim the laser pointer

Sensor values appear on the canvas during recording. Full setup →
