Chapter 1: Getting Started

1.1 What is NoorCast?

NoorCast is a free, browser-based, multi-camera screen recorder specifically designed for teachers creating tutorial videos with robots and micro:bits. It combines screen capture, webcam feeds, microphone input, and micro:bit sensor data into a single recording — all from your browser, without installing anything.

Unlike traditional screen recorders, NoorCast was built for education. It understands that a teacher explaining robotics needs to show their screen (code), their robot (camera), their face (webcam), and their micro:bit sensor readings — all at once, switching between layouts seamlessly.

Key Facts

  • 100% local — nothing leaves your computer. No cloud, no accounts, no tracking.
  • Zero install — just open a URL in Chrome or Edge. Works offline after first load.
  • Zero dependencies — no npm, no build step. One HTML + one JS + one CSS file.
  • 200+ features — from basic recording to AI co-hosts and instant replay.
  • Trilingual — French (default), English, Arabic (with full RTL support).
  • PWA installable — add to your desktop/home screen like a native app.

1.4 Simple vs Pro Mode

NoorCast offers two modes to match your experience level:

🎓 Simple Mode

Designed for beginners and young students. Hides advanced tools to prevent overwhelm:

  • Right sidebar (scenes) auto-collapsed
  • More Tools overflow menu hidden
  • Source style popup simplified
  • Pause/Marker buttons hidden
  • Advanced settings sections hidden
  • Only essential recording controls visible

Perfect for: First-time users, elementary school students, quick recordings.

🚀 Pro Mode

Full access to every feature. All panels and tools visible:

  • Right sidebar with scenes always visible
  • All toolbar buttons accessible
  • More Tools with 30+ additional features
  • Full source style popup (skins, crop, filters, shadows)
  • All settings sections visible
  • Advanced overlays and effects available

Perfect for: Teachers, power users, detailed tutorials.

Switch between modes anytime: open Settings (⚙️ gear icon) → click the mode switch button at the top.

The mode is persisted in localStorage. NoorCast remembers your choice across sessions.

1.5 Your First Recording — Step by Step

Add a screen source. In the left sidebar, click the blue 🖥 + Add button under "Screen". Chrome will ask which screen, window, or tab to share. Pick one and click "Share".
Add a camera. Select your webcam from the "Camera" dropdown, then click the green 🎥 + Add button. Grant camera permission if prompted. Your face appears as a small circle in the bottom-right of the canvas.
Select a microphone. Choose your mic from the "Mic" dropdown. It activates immediately — you'll see the LED meter in the recording bar respond to your voice. A mic check message confirms it's working.
Choose a scene. In the right sidebar (Pro mode), click a scene preset: 💻 Code (screen + face), 🤖 Mecha (camera + face), or any other layout. The canvas rearranges your sources instantly.
Press Record. Click the big red 🔴 REC button (or press R). A 3-2-1 countdown plays with beep tones, then "GO!" flashes with speed lines. Recording begins.
Present your tutorial. Switch scenes with keys 1-7. Use L for laser pointer, F to freeze the screen, D to draw. Press M to add chapter markers.
Stop recording. Click ⏹ Stop or press R again. The take panel appears with your video preview and download options.
Download. Click ⬇️ Download to save your .webm file. Or explore post-recording tools: Instant Replay, Auto Thumbnail, Quality Score, Transcript, Export Flyer.
The entire recording stays on your computer. Nothing is uploaded anywhere. The video file is created locally in your browser.

Chapter 3: Sources — Adding Content to Your Canvas

3.1 Screen Capture

Click the blue 🖥 + Add button. Chrome presents three capture options:

  • Entire Screen — captures everything visible on your monitor
  • Window — captures a specific application window
  • Browser Tab — captures a specific Chrome tab (includes audio)

After selecting, the screen source appears full-size on the canvas. You can resize and reposition it.

Capturing a browser tab allows you to include the tab's audio (e.g., a YouTube video or an online IDE) in your recording.

Screen Audio

When sharing a tab or screen with "Share audio" checked, the audio is automatically routed through NoorCast's volume ducking system. When you speak into your mic, the screen audio is automatically lowered so your voice is clear.

3.2 Camera

Select your camera from the dropdown list, then click the green 🎥 + Add button. You can add multiple cameras:

  • Built-in laptop webcam
  • USB external webcams (multiple supported)
  • iPhone/Mac Continuity Camera
  • Virtual cameras (OBS Virtual Camera, etc.)

Each camera appears as a source on the canvas with a default size and position determined by the active scene.

Mirror Webcam

By default, webcams are not mirrored. Toggle mirroring per source via right-click → Mirror, or in the style popup.

3.3 Microphone

Select a mic from the "Mic" dropdown. It activates immediately — no "Add" button needed. Features:

  • Auto mic check — when you select a mic, NoorCast monitors audio for 3 seconds and shows a status message: "🎤 Speak to test..." → "✅ Mic OK!" or "⚠️ Nothing detected"
  • Noise gate — configurable in Settings. Mutes the mic below a threshold (default -50 dB) to eliminate background noise
  • Gain boost — adjustable 0-4× gain slider in Settings
  • Volume ducking — when you speak, screen audio is automatically reduced

3.4 Images (Paste & Drop)

Two ways to add images:

  • Paste: Copy an image anywhere (web, file explorer, screenshot tool), then press Ctrl+V in NoorCast. The image appears as a source on the canvas.
  • Drag & Drop: Drag an image file from your desktop onto the canvas. A green dashed border highlights the drop zone. Supports PNG, JPG, GIF, SVG, WebP.

3.7 Drag, Resize, and Position

Moving Sources

Click and drag any source to reposition it on the canvas. Sources snap to:

  • 7 anchor points (corners + edge centers + center) within 60px
  • Other sources — alignment guides appear (pink dashed lines) when edges or centers align with another source (8px snap threshold)
  • Grid — hold Alt while dragging for a 48px grid. Or enable the configurable snap grid in Settings.

Resizing Sources

When selected, 8 circular handles appear around the source:

  • 4 corner handles (12px radius, white with accent border) — resize proportionally (aspect ratio locked by default)
  • 4 edge midpoint handles (8px radius) — stretch width OR height independently
  • Hold Shift while dragging a corner to free resize (unlock aspect ratio)

The cursor changes when hovering near handles: diagonal arrows (↗↙) for corners, vertical (↕) or horizontal (↔) for edges.

Hover Feedback

When you hover over any source (without clicking), a subtle accent-colored bounding box appears, showing you which source is under your cursor.

Right-Click Context Menu

Right-click any source for a Canva-style context menu with: Hide, Pin, Lock, Duplicate, Shape submenu, Layer ordering (Forward/Backward/Front/Back), PiP popout, Mirror, Mirror Duplicate.

3.8 Visual Crop Tool

Select a source → click ✂️ in the toolbar. A crosshair cursor appears over the canvas.

Draw a rectangle by clicking and dragging over the region you want to keep.
The area outside your rectangle darkens (semi-transparent black overlay).
Press Enter to apply the crop, or Escape to cancel.

The crop is stored as fractional values (cropTop, cropBottom, cropLeft, cropRight) and applied during rendering. You can fine-tune the crop later using the sliders in the style popup.

3.9 Source Fly-In Animation

When you add any source (screen, camera, image, shape), it flies in from the bottom with a smooth scale-up animation (400ms ease-out cubic curve). This gives visual feedback that the source was successfully added.

Chapter 4: The Canvas — Your Creative Space

4.1 Canvas Basics

The canvas is a 1920×1080 pixel HTML5 canvas element that composites all your sources, overlays, effects, and UI elements into a single output. This is what gets recorded.

  • Background color — configurable in Settings (default: black). Color picker available.
  • Background pattern — 15 animated patterns available (see Chapter 15)
  • Aspect ratio — default 16:9. Can be changed to 4:3, 1:1, 9:16 (portrait) in Settings.
  • Zoom — Ctrl+scroll to zoom the canvas view. Not the same as the recording zoom (Z key).

4.2 Layer Order

Sources are drawn in the order they appear in the sources list (bottom of list = drawn last = on top). To change layer order:

  • Right-click a source → Forward/Backward/Front/Back
  • Or drag to reorder in the sidebar source list

When hovering a source, a small layer badge appears showing its position: "#2/4" (layer 2 of 4 total).

5.3 Skin Technical Details

  • Skins are rendered using the CanvasRenderingContext2D API — pure drawing, no image files.
  • An evenodd clip ensures frame fills don't cover the video content — only the border area is drawn.
  • Some skins have a foreground overlay pass (e.g., Robot scan lines, CCTV timestamps) that draws ON TOP of the video.
  • Animated skins (Flame, Neon, DNA, Force Field, Warp) update every frame using Date.now().
  • Skins are persisted in custom scene snapshots — when you save a custom scene, each source's skin is saved and restored.

A scene is a layout preset that defines the position, size, shape, and visibility of each source. Switching scenes instantly rearranges your canvas without stopping the recording.

Use case: During a robotics tutorial, switch from "Code" (screen full + face PIP) to "Robot" (camera full + face PIP) to "Code+Robot" (split screen) by pressing a single key.

Chapter 7: Recording

7.1 Starting a Recording

Standard Start (with countdown)

Click the 🔴 REC button or press R. A 3-2-1 countdown plays:

  • Each number appears as a large digit (12rem) on the canvas with a scale pulse animation
  • Rising pitch beep tones: 440 Hz → 660 Hz → 880 Hz (getting higher as the countdown nears zero)
  • After "1", a "GO!" text flashes with a 1100 Hz tone + speed lines burst

Chapter 9: micro:bit Integration

9.1 Overview

NoorCast connects to a BBC micro:bit via Web Bluetooth (Chrome/Edge only). The micro:bit becomes a physical remote control for your recording session, and its sensor data is captured alongside the video.

9.2 Connecting

Flash the NoorCast firmware to your micro:bit via MakeCode (click "Flash Firmware First" in the micro:bit sidebar tab).
Click 📡 Connect in the micro:bit sidebar tab.
A browser Bluetooth pairing dialog appears. Select your micro:bit (named "BBC micro:bit [xxxxx]" or "uBit [xxxxx]").
Once connected, the status shows "✅ Connected" and sensor values begin streaming.
Web Bluetooth requires Chrome or Edge on desktop. Not supported in Firefox, Safari, or mobile browsers.

9.3 Controls

D-Pad

Arrow buttons send directional commands to the micro:bit:

↑CMD:UP←CMD:LEFT
↓CMD:DOWN→CMD:RIGHT
●CMD:CLEAR (center)🔥CMD:FIRE

D-pad commands are movement commands — they don't control servo angles. They're independent from the servo sliders.

Servo Sliders

Two sliders (Pan: 0-180° and Tilt: 0-180°) send P: and TI: UART commands. These are synced bidirectionally with:

  • The sidebar servo sliders
  • The Pilot scene overlay sliders
  • The servo gauge canvas overlay

LED 5×5 Grid

Click cells to toggle them on/off, then click 📡 Send to transmit the pattern to the micro:bit. The grid also mirrors incoming LED data from the micro:bit — if the micro:bit sends a LEDS: UART message, the grid updates automatically.

Joystick

Available in the Pilot scene overlay. Two modes:

  • CMD mode (blue knob) — drag direction sends CMD:LEFT/RIGHT/UP/DOWN commands. Throttled to 200ms. Releases to CMD:CLEAR.
  • Servo mode (green knob) — drag position maps to pan (0-180) and tilt (0-180) angles. Updates servo gauges in real-time.

Toggle between modes by clicking the CMD/Servo button above the joystick.

9.4 Sensor Overlay

When enabled (More Tools → Fun → 📊 Sensors), the sensor overlay renders on the canvas showing:

  • Accelerometer X, Y, Z values
  • Temperature
  • Light level
  • Sound level
  • Compass heading
  • LED state (mirrored 5×5 grid)

The overlay is draggable and resizable (drag corners). Double-click to cycle opacity (100→75→50→25→100%). Position and opacity are persisted in localStorage.

The overlay renders even without a micro:bit connection (showing zero values). This allows you to test the layout.

9.5 Servo Gauges

When enabled (More Tools → Fun → 🎛 Gauges), two semicircular gauges render on the canvas showing the current Pan and Tilt angles. They update when:

  • You move the servo sliders
  • You use the joystick in Servo mode
  • The micro:bit sends angle updates

Gauges show even without a connection — they appear as soon as you move a slider from the default 90°.

  • Camera fills the canvas (robot view)

Chapter 14: Audio Features

14.1 Audio Pipeline

NoorCast's audio system uses the Web Audio API:

  • Mic input → MicBoost (gain + noise gate) → analyser (VU meter + voice detection) → recording destination
  • Screen audio (if shared) → VolumeDuck gain node → recording destination
  • Background music (if enabled) → recording destination + speaker output
  • Sound pad → speaker output only (not in recording unless routed)

14.2 Mic Boost & Noise Gate

Settings → Recording section:

  • Gain: 0.0× to 4.0× amplification slider
  • Noise gate: -60 dB to -20 dB threshold slider. Audio below this threshold is muted (eliminates fan noise, room hum).

14.3 Volume Ducking

When you speak into the mic, screen audio (from a shared tab/screen) is automatically reduced by 12 dB. This ensures your voice is always clear over background content. Toggle in Settings.

14.4 LED Audio Meter

The 10-segment LED meter in the recording bar shows your real-time audio level:

  • Segments 1-6: green (normal range)
  • Segments 7-8: yellow (loud)
  • Segments 9-10: red (clipping — too loud)

14.5 Mic Check

When you select a mic from the dropdown, an automatic 3-second test runs:

  • "🎤 Speak to test..." message appears
  • AudioContext is resumed (Chrome autoplay policy)
  • Analyser monitors RMS level for 3 seconds
  • If sound detected (RMS > 0.02): "✅ Mic OK!"
  • If no sound: "⚠️ Nothing detected"
  • Message auto-dismisses after 4 seconds

Chapter 19: Privacy & Security

19.1 100% Local

NoorCast processes everything in your browser. Nothing is uploaded, streamed, or sent to any server. The complete data flow:

  • Video streams → composed on HTML5 Canvas → encoded via MediaRecorder → saved as a local Blob → downloaded to your disk
  • Audio → processed via Web Audio API (gain, gate, analysis) → mixed into recording → local file
  • micro:bit data → received via Web Bluetooth (local radio) → displayed on canvas → saved as CSV locally
  • Settings → stored in browser localStorage → never transmitted

19.2 No External Requests

Since v0.7.159, all fonts are self-hosted as WOFF2 files. NoorCast makes zero outbound network requests from JavaScript. The only network activity is:

  • Initial page load (downloading HTML/JS/CSS/fonts from the hosting server)
  • Service Worker cache operations (caching the app shell for offline use)
  • SpeechRecognition API (browser-handled, sends audio to Google/Microsoft for processing — this is the browser's built-in speech service, not NoorCast's)

19.3 No Accounts, No Analytics

  • No user accounts or login
  • No analytics scripts (no Google Analytics, no Mixpanel, nothing)
  • No cookies (only localStorage for preferences)
  • No telemetry or crash reporting
  • GDPR/CCPA compliant by design (no personal data collected)

19.4 What's Stored Locally

NoorCast uses ~100 localStorage keys to remember your preferences:

  • Language, theme, mode (Simple/Pro)
  • All toggle states (VoiceFx, XP, SoundPad, Co-host, etc.)
  • Source positions and scene layouts
  • Brand logo, slogan, watermark settings
  • XP count, level, daily challenge state
  • Recording history (metadata only — no video data)
  • Robot choreography sequence
  • Teleprompter script

All of this can be cleared at once: Settings → Maintenance → 💥 Clear Cache.

Chapter 20: FAQ & Troubleshooting

20.1 Recording Issues

Q: My recording is 0 bytes / empty

This usually happens when using the File System Access API (disk streaming) to save directly to a file. The recording IS saved to disk, but the in-memory chunks array is empty. Since v0.7.193, NoorCast handles this correctly — the take panel and post-recording features work even with disk-streamed recordings.

Q: The video has no audio

Make sure you selected a microphone from the Mic dropdown. Check that the LED meter in the recording bar responds to your voice. If the AudioContext is suspended (Chrome autoplay policy), click anywhere on the page to resume it.

Q: Recording stutters or drops frames

Reduce the number of active effects (canvas pets, animated backgrounds, neon border). Close other browser tabs to free up CPU. NoorCast targets 30fps — complex compositions may drop frames on older hardware.

20.2 Captions Issues

Q: Captions aren't showing any text

Check that:

  • You're using Chrome or Edge (SpeechRecognition not supported in Firefox/Safari)
  • A microphone is active (selected in the dropdown)
  • You're speaking loudly enough for the browser to detect speech
  • Check the Activity Log for "💬 Captions started" — if you see an error, it will explain why

Q: Captions are in the wrong language

The caption language follows NoorCast's UI language setting (Settings → Language). If you set English, captions use en-US. Set French for fr-FR, Arabic for ar-SA.

20.3 micro:bit Issues

Q: Can't find my micro:bit in the Bluetooth pairing dialog

  • Make sure the micro:bit has the NoorCast firmware flashed
  • The micro:bit must be powered on and not connected to another device
  • Try restarting the micro:bit (press the reset button on the back)
  • Web Bluetooth only works in Chrome/Edge on desktop

Q: micro:bit disconnects frequently

NoorCast has an auto-reconnect system (3 attempts). If the micro:bit disconnects, it will try to reconnect automatically. Keep the micro:bit within Bluetooth range (~10 meters).

20.4 General Issues

Q: The app won't load / shows a blank page

Clear the service worker cache: Chrome → Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data → Cached images and files. Then reload.

Q: I lost all my settings

If you cleared your browser data or used private/incognito mode, localStorage is wiped. There's no recovery. Use Settings → Export Session to back up your settings regularly.

Q: Focus mode is stuck — I can't access any buttons

Press Escape to exit Focus mode. Or click the semi-transparent "✕ Exit Focus" button at the top-right corner.

Q: Where is the Fun section?

Click ⋯ More in the toolbar → scroll down to the "Fun" group. The More Tools popup is scrollable (max-height 70vh).
