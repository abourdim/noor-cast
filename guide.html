🚀 Quick Start (2 minutes)

  1. Open index.html in Chrome or Edge (desktop only)
  2. Grant camera + microphone permissions when prompted
  3. Pick a template: 📚 Full lesson, 🤖 Robot demo, 🔧 Fix-it, or 🤖 Robot Tuto
  4. Hit the big red REC button
  5. Follow the template steps — each click switches scenes and drops a chapter marker
  6. Press Stop when done
  7. Download your .webm video + .vtt chapter file

📹 Adding Sources

Screen share

Click + Add under Screen. Pick a window, tab, or entire screen.

Webcam

Select your camera from the dropdown, then click + Add. Drag and resize on the canvas.

Microphone

Select your mic from the dropdown. Audio is recorded automatically.

Image

Paste an image from clipboard (Ctrl+V) to add it as a static source.

📱 Option A: Continuity Camera (Mac + iPhone)

Zero setup — just works if both devices share the same Apple ID and Wi-Fi.

  1. Sign into the same Apple ID on both devices
  2. Connect to the same Wi-Fi network
  3. Open TutoCast in Chrome on your Mac
  4. Click + Camera — your iPhone appears as "iPhone Camera"
  5. Select it and click + Add

📲 Option B: DroidCam

Free. Works on any phone + Windows/Mac. Landscape only — use Iriun if you need portrait.

On your phone

  1. Install DroidCam: App Store (iPhone) · Play Store (Android)
  2. Open the app — note the IP address and port shown on screen

On your computer

  1. Download DroidCam Client: dev47apps.com/droidcam
  2. Install it — adds a virtual camera driver
  3. Open DroidCam Client, enter the phone's IP, click Start

In TutoCast

  1. Click + Camera — "DroidCam Source" appears in the dropdown
  2. Select it, click + Add
  3. Drag and resize on the canvas

📡 Option C: Iriun Webcam Recommended for portrait

Free. Works on Windows/Mac/Linux. Supports portrait (vertical) mode — DroidCam does not.

On your phone

  1. Install Iriun: App Store (iPhone) · Play Store (Android)
  2. Open the app — it shows "Waiting for connection..."

On your computer

  1. Download from iriun.com: Windows · Mac · Linux (Ubuntu)
  2. Install — adds a virtual camera driver
  3. No need to open the desktop app — the driver runs automatically

Connecting via Wi-Fi

  1. Same Wi-Fi network on both devices
  2. Open Iriun on the phone — it auto-discovers your computer
  3. Phone shows "Connected" with a live preview

Connecting via USB (recommended)

  1. Plug phone into computer with USB cable
  2. iPhone: trust the computer · Android: enable USB debugging
  3. Open Iriun on the phone — connects via USB (faster, no lag)

In TutoCast

  1. Click + Camera → select "Iriun Webcam"
  2. Click + Add

Portrait (vertical) mode

Just hold your phone vertically. Iriun sends the portrait feed automatically — no rotation needed. Resize the source in TutoCast to a tall narrow rectangle.

Settings in the phone app

  • Camera: tap to switch front/back
  • Resolution: 640p default is fine; 1080p uses more CPU
  • Orientation: auto-detects when you rotate
  • Mirror: toggle if image is flipped

Troubleshooting

  • "Waiting for connection": check that the desktop driver is installed (system tray on Windows, menu bar on Mac)
  • Lag/stuttering: switch to USB, or lower resolution
  • Not in dropdown: restart Chrome after installing the driver
  • Black screen: close other apps using the camera (Zoom, Teams)

🎥 Option D: Camo Pro quality

Best image quality. Manual controls for zoom, exposure, focus, white balance, ISO. Supports portrait + landscape.

On your phone

  1. Install Camo: App Store (iPhone) · Play Store (Android)
  2. Open the app — live camera preview with toolbar

On your computer

  1. Download Camo Studio: Windows · Mac (no Linux)
  2. Install — adds a "Camo" virtual camera
  3. Open Camo Studio — it shows a pairing screen

Connecting via USB (recommended)

  1. Plug phone into computer
  2. iPhone: trust the computer · Android: enable USB debugging
  3. Camo Studio shows the live feed immediately

Connecting via Wi-Fi

  1. Same Wi-Fi network on both devices
  2. In Camo Studio, select your phone from the device dropdown

In TutoCast

  1. Click + Camera → select "Camo"
  2. Click + Add

Troubleshooting

  • Not in dropdown: restart Chrome after installing Camo Studio
  • Black screen: make sure Camo Studio is open on the computer (required, unlike Iriun)
  • Lag: switch to USB
  • Watermark: upgrade to Pro, or use TutoCast's Crop sliders to crop it out

General tip: USB beats Wi-Fi for stability. Disable phone auto-lock. A cheap phone tripod makes a huge difference.

Using the Robot Tuto template

  1. On first launch, pick 🤖 Robot Tuto
  2. TutoCast creates 5 steps: Intro → Code → Robot → Sensors → Recap
  3. Click each step to switch scenes during recording
  4. Each switch drops a chapter marker in the video

🔌 Connecting micro:bit

TutoCast reads your micro:bit sensors live via Web Bluetooth.

  1. Click 📡 Connect micro:bit in the left sidebar
  2. Pair your micro:bit in the browser popup
  3. Accelerometer data appears in real time
  4. Button A = zoom in · Button B = drop a chapter marker
  5. Tilting the micro:bit drives the laser pointer on screen

MakeCode program (flash to your micro:bit)

bluetooth.startAccelerometerService()
bluetooth.startButtonService()
basic.forever(() => {
    basic.pause(100)
})

Enable "🤖 Overlay auto si le robot bouge fort" in Settings → Recording to show accelerometer values on the canvas during recording.

For teachers who film themselves building and assembling robots on a desk.

Cheap overhead mounts

  • Phone gooseneck clamp (~8 EUR) — clips to desk, bends over workspace. Best option.
  • Selfie stick + tape — quick and dirty
  • Book stand leaning over desk with phone resting on it

Assembly filming tips

  1. Portrait mode (Iriun) for overhead — shows more vertical desk space
  2. Good lighting matters more than camera quality — desk lamp on the workspace
  3. Freeze + Draw (F then D) — freeze overhead, annotate components
  4. Drop markers (M) at each step — "Step 1: Solder motor driver"
  5. Rename "You" scene to "My Hands" — double-click the label
  6. Text stickers for part labels — ✏️ Add Text → "Motor Driver"

Example workflow

  1. Intro (face cam) — "Today we're building a line-following robot!"
  2. Parts (overhead) — show all components laid out
  3. Build (overhead + close-up) — assemble step by step, drop markers
  4. Code (screen share) — write the program
  5. Test (robot cam) — run the robot
  6. Recap (face cam) — wrap up

Privacy for camera-shy teachers

  • 🤖 Avatar mode — cartoon face replaces camera, mouth animates with voice
  • 🔲 Privacy blur — heavy blur, face unrecognizable but presence felt
  • Or simply skip the face camera — screen + robot + mic works great

Toggle per-source in 🎨 Style → Privacy.

Starting

Click the big red REC button or press R. A 3-2-1 countdown plays first.

During recording

  • M = drop a chapter marker

  • 1-6 = switch scenes

After recording

  • Trim: cut start/end of video
  • Silence trim: auto-remove pauses
  • Take notes: journal what went well
  • Rate: 1-5 stars

⚙ Settings

Click the ⚙ gear icon (top-right). Organized into 5 sections:

  • General: Language, theme, canvas background, snap-to-grid
  • Recording → Capture: countdown, mirror webcam, output format, aspect ratio
  • Recording → Overlays: clock, REC indicator, captions, letterbox, vignette
  • Recording → Audio: SFX, mic boost, noise gate, volume ducking
  • Recording → Behavior: auto-pause, transitions, auto-save
  • Logo: upload school logo, adjust size/opacity/filter, 3 brand presets
  • Slogan: scrolling text, watermark, badge card URL
  • Ticker: custom scrolling messages

💡 Tips for Great Tutorials

  1. Do a dry run — test all scene switches before recording
  2. Use the teleprompter (T) — paste your script, it auto-scrolls
  3. Drop markers (M) — viewers get a clickable chapter timeline
  4. Freeze + Draw (F then D) — annotate code with arrows and circles
  5. Keep takes short — 5-10 minutes, re-record freely
  6. Use silence trim — removes "umm..." pauses automatically
  7. Check the dashboard — track your streak and badges

🔧 Troubleshooting

Camera not in dropdown

Close other apps using the camera (Zoom, Teams). Refresh the page. Check browser permissions: lock icon → Camera → Allow.

Phone camera not appearing

Same Wi-Fi? Driver installed? Try USB instead. Restart Chrome after installing a new driver.

0-byte recording

Check the activity log. Try switching output format. Ensure at least one video source is active.

micro:bit won't connect

Chrome/Edge only (no Firefox). Bluetooth enabled? Flash the MakeCode BLE program above.

Video is choppy

Close browser tabs. Lower resolution in Settings. Disable unused overlays.
