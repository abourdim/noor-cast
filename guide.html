1 Getting Started

1.1 What is NoorCast?

NoorCast is a free, browser-based, multi-camera screen recorder specifically designed for teachers creating tutorial videos with robots and micro:bits. It combines screen capture, webcam feeds, microphone input, and micro:bit sensor data into a single recording — all from your browser, without installing anything.

Unlike traditional screen recorders, NoorCast was built for education. It understands that a teacher explaining robotics needs to show their screen (code), their robot (camera), their face (webcam), and their micro:bit sensor readings — all at once, switching between layouts seamlessly.

Key Facts

  • 100% local — nothing leaves your computer. No cloud, no accounts, no tracking.
  • Zero install — just open a URL in Chrome or Edge. Works offline after first load.
  • Zero dependencies — no npm, no build step. One HTML + one JS + one CSS file.
  • 200+ features — from basic recording to AI co-hosts and instant replay.
  • Trilingual — French (default), English, Arabic (with full RTL support).
  • PWA installable — add to your desktop/home screen like a native app.

1.3 Opening NoorCast

Navigate to abourdim.github.io/noor-cast in Chrome or Edge.
The splash screen appears briefly (2 seconds) with the NoorCast logo and Bismillah greeting.
On first launch, a mode picker appears. Choose between Simple (less UI) and Pro (full features). See Section 1.4.
Optionally, a template picker appears offering guided lesson templates. See Chapter 7.
The main interface loads. You're ready to add sources and start recording.

1.4 Simple vs Pro Mode

🎓 Simple Mode

Designed for beginners and young students:

  • Right sidebar (scenes) auto-collapsed
  • More Tools overflow menu hidden
  • Pause/Marker buttons hidden
  • Advanced settings sections hidden

🚀 Pro Mode

Full access to every feature:

  • Right sidebar with scenes visible
  • All toolbar buttons accessible
  • Full source style popup
  • All settings sections visible

Switch anytime: Settings → mode switch button at the top.

The mode is persisted in localStorage. NoorCast remembers your choice across sessions.

1.5 Your First Recording

Add a screen source. In the left sidebar, click the blue 🖥 + Add button. Chrome asks which screen/window/tab to share. See Chapter 4.
Add a camera. Select your webcam from the dropdown, click green 🎥 + Add. For phone-as-camera setup, see Chapter 3.
Select a microphone. Choose from the "Mic" dropdown. A mic check runs automatically.
Choose a scene. Click a scene preset in the right sidebar: 💻 Code, 🤖 Mecha, etc.
Press Record. Click 🔴 REC or press R. A 3-2-1 countdown plays, then recording begins.
Present. Switch scenes with 1-7. Use overlays: L laser, F freeze, D draw. Press M for markers.
Stop. Click ⏹ Stop or press R. The take panel opens.
Download. Click ⬇️ Download. Or use post-recording tools: Instant Replay, Thumbnail, Transcript, Quality Score.

3 Camera Setup — Detailed Guide

NoorCast supports multiple simultaneous cameras. This chapter covers every way to add a camera, from built-in webcams to using your phone.

3.1 Built-in Laptop Webcam

In the left sidebar, find the Camera dropdown.
Your built-in webcam appears as "FaceTime HD Camera" (Mac), "Integrated Camera" (Windows), or similar.
Select it from the dropdown.
Click the green 🎥 + Add button.
Grant camera permission when the browser asks.
Your face appears on the canvas. By default, it's placed as a small circle in the bottom-right (PIP position).
If the camera feed is flipped (text appears mirrored), right-click the source → Mirror to fix it.

3.2 USB External Webcams

Plug in a USB webcam. It appears in the Camera dropdown automatically (may take 2-3 seconds).

Using Multiple USB Cameras

NoorCast supports unlimited simultaneous cameras. Add them one at a time:

  1. Select Camera 1 from dropdown → click Add
  2. Select Camera 2 from dropdown → click Add
  3. Repeat for Camera 3, 4, etc.

Each camera becomes a separate source on the canvas that you can position, resize, and style independently.

3.3 Phone as Camera (Wireless Webcam)

Use your iPhone or Android phone as a wireless camera. Several options:

Option A: iPhone Continuity Camera (Mac only)

If you have a Mac running macOS Ventura (13) or later and an iPhone running iOS 16+:

Make sure both devices are signed into the same Apple ID and on the same Wi-Fi network.
Bluetooth must be enabled on both devices.
In NoorCast, open the Camera dropdown. Your iPhone appears as a camera option.
Select it and click Add. Your iPhone's camera feed appears on the canvas.
Place your iPhone near the robot for a dedicated robot cam. Use your laptop's built-in webcam for your face.

Option B: DroidCam (Windows/Mac + Android/iPhone)

Install DroidCam on your phone (free on App Store / Play Store).
Install the DroidCam client on your computer (Windows or Mac).
Connect your phone and computer to the same Wi-Fi network.
Open DroidCam on your phone — it shows an IP address and port.
Open DroidCam client on your computer and enter the IP/port. Click Start.
In NoorCast, the Camera dropdown now shows "DroidCam" as an option. Select and Add.

Option C: EpocCam (iPhone + Windows/Mac)

Install EpocCam on your iPhone.
Install the EpocCam driver on your computer.
Both devices must be on the same Wi-Fi or connected via USB.
Open EpocCam on your iPhone.
In NoorCast, "EpocCam" appears in the Camera dropdown. Select and Add.

Option D: OBS Virtual Camera

If you already use OBS Studio, you can:

In OBS, set up your phone camera feed (via NDI, Spout, or capture card).
Click "Start Virtual Camera" in OBS.
In NoorCast, "OBS Virtual Camera" appears in the dropdown. Select and Add.

3.4 Camera Angles for Tutorials

For a robotics tutorial, you typically need 2-3 camera angles:

📷 Face Camera

Position: Eye level, looking at you.

Purpose: Shows your face while explaining. Builds connection with students.

Scene: Appears as a small circle (PIP) in most scenes. Full screen in "Solo" scene.

Tips: Good lighting on your face. Neutral background. Look at the camera, not the screen.

🤖 Robot Camera

Position: Overhead or angled view of the robot/breadboard.

Purpose: Shows the physical robot, wiring, or components.

Scene: Full screen in "Mecha" scene. Right half in "Code+Mecha".

Tips: Use a phone on a tripod or clamp. Good lighting on the workspace. Avoid shadows from your hands.

🎛 Sensor Camera

Position: Pointing at a display, oscilloscope, or sensor readout.

Purpose: Shows real-time sensor data or test equipment.

Scene: Full screen in "Sensors" scene.

Tips: Use a second USB webcam or phone. Frame the display tightly.

🖥 Screen (not a camera)

Position: N/A — captures your computer screen.

Purpose: Shows code, IDE, browser, or presentation.

Scene: Full screen in "Code" scene. Left half in "Code+Mecha".

Tips: Increase font size in your IDE. Close notification popups. Use a clean desktop.

3.5 Camera Troubleshooting

ProblemSolution
Camera not appearing in dropdownCheck USB connection. Try a different USB port. Refresh the page. Make sure no other app is using the camera.
Black screen after adding cameraAnother app may be using the camera. Close Zoom/Teams/Skype. Refresh NoorCast.
Camera permission deniedClick the camera icon in the browser's address bar → Allow. Refresh the page.
Wrong camera selectedRemove the source (click ✕), then add the correct camera from the dropdown.
Camera image is mirroredRight-click the source → Mirror. Or: Style popup → Flip H.
Camera feed is laggyClose other browser tabs. Reduce the number of active canvas pets and background patterns.
Phone camera disconnectsKeep the phone's screen on (disable auto-lock). Stay on the same Wi-Fi network. Keep DroidCam/EpocCam in foreground.
NoorCast captures cameras at 1280×720 (720p) by default. The canvas composites everything at 1920×1080 (1080p) regardless of individual camera resolution.

4 Sources — Adding Content

4.1 Screen Capture

Click the blue 🖥 + Add button. Chrome presents three options:

  • Entire Screen — captures everything on your monitor
  • Window — captures a specific application window
  • Browser Tab — captures a specific Chrome tab (includes tab audio)
Capturing a browser tab allows you to include the tab's audio (YouTube, online IDE) in your recording. The audio is automatically ducked when you speak. See Audio chapter.

4.2 Camera

For detailed camera setup including phones, see Chapter 3.

4.3 Microphone

Select from the "Mic" dropdown. Auto-activates immediately. Features:

  • Auto mic check — 3-second test: "🎤 Speak to test..." → "✅ Mic OK!" or "⚠️ Nothing detected". See Section 17.5.
  • Noise gate — mutes below threshold (-50 dB default). Configurable in Settings.
  • Gain boost — 0-4× slider in Settings.
  • Volume ducking — screen audio reduced when you speak. See Section 17.3.

4.4 Images (Paste & Drag)

  • Paste: Ctrl+V — paste any image from clipboard
  • Drag & Drop: drag an image file from desktop onto the canvas. Green dashed border highlights the drop zone. Supports PNG, JPG, GIF, SVG, WebP.

4.5 Shapes

Right sidebar → 🎨 Ajouter → Shapes. 15 options: Rectangle, Circle, Rounded, Pill, Hexagon, Octagon, Diamond, Star, Heart, Full bg, Dark bar, White bar, Red dot, Green dot, Dark card.

Each shape is a canvas source — resize, reposition, style with skins, borders, and shadows.

4.7 Resize Handles

8 circular handles when selected:

  • 4 corner handles (12px) — proportional resize (aspect locked by default)
  • 4 edge handles (8px) — stretch width OR height only
  • Hold Shift + corner drag = free resize (unlock aspect ratio)

Cursor changes on hover: ↗↙ for corners, ↕ or ↔ for edges, move cursor for body.

Hover any source to see a subtle bounding box outline.

4.9 Source Fly-In Animation

5 Canvas & Source Styling

5.1 Canvas Basics

  • Resolution: 1920×1080 (16:9). Configurable aspect ratio in Settings: 4:3, 1:1, 9:16.
  • Background color: Settings → color picker (default: black).
  • Background pattern: 15 animated patterns. See Chapter 18.
  • Zoom: Ctrl+scroll to zoom the canvas view.

5.2 Layer Order

Sources draw bottom-to-top (last in list = on top). Change via right-click → Forward/Backward/Front/Back. Hover shows layer badge: "#2/4".

Crosshair cursor appears. Draw a rectangle over the region to keep.
Area outside darkens (semi-transparent overlay).

5.5 Stickers & Text Overlays

Right sidebar → 🎨 Ajouter:

  • Quick stickers — preset text overlays (auto-fade 4s)
  • Custom text — permanent text, any content
  • Emoji palette — 32 emoji overlays
  • 🏷 Animated stickers — 20 stickers with random animation: bounce, spin, pulse, wiggle, or float. See Section 14.3.

Text overlays support: 6 fonts, 4 transparency modes, outline stroke (0-14px), drag, resize, rotate, duplicate (Ctrl+D).

10 micro:bit Integration

10.1 Connecting

Flash NoorCast firmware via MakeCode (click "Flash Firmware First" in sidebar).
Click 📡 Connect in the micro:bit sidebar tab.
Select your micro:bit from the Bluetooth dialog.
Status shows "✅ Connected". Sensor data streams.
Requires Chrome or Edge on desktop. Not Firefox/Safari/mobile.

10.2 Controls

D-Pad

↑↓←→ = CMD:UP/DOWN/LEFT/RIGHT. ● = CMD:CLEAR. 🔥 = CMD:FIRE. Movement commands (not servo).

Servo Sliders

Pan (0-180°) + Tilt (0-180°). Synced with Pilot overlay + canvas gauges.

LED 5×5

Click cells to toggle. 📡 Send transmits. Mirrors incoming LEDS: data from micro:bit.

Sensors

Accelerometer X/Y/Z, temp, light, sound, compass. Overlay on canvas (toggle: Fun → Sensors).

10.3 BLE Logging

All UART messages in Activity Log: → CMD:LEFT (sent, green), ← ACC:1,2,3 (received, yellow). Filter with TX/RX buttons.

10.4 Robot Choreography

Fun → 🤖 Choreo: record all UART commands with timestamps. ▶️ Replay plays back in sync. Persisted in localStorage.

11 Pilot Scene & Overlay

Scene 6 🎮 Pilot: camera full + face PIP + draggable micro:bit overlay at bottom-left containing:

  • D-pad — arrow buttons + center + fire
  • Servo sliders — Pan + Tilt with value display
  • Joystick — two modes: CMD (blue, direction commands) / Servo (green, angle control). Toggle via button.
  • LED 5×5 grid — mirrors micro:bit LEDs. 📡 Send / 🗑 Clear.

Overlay is draggable (grab "🎮 micro:bit" header). Position persisted. Auto-hides on scene switch. All controls synced with sidebar.

22 FAQ & Troubleshooting

Recording

0-byte recordingIf using disk streaming, this is normal — file is saved to disk. The take panel works correctly since v0.7.193.
No audioSelect a mic from dropdown. Check LED meter responds. Click page to resume AudioContext.
StutteringReduce active effects (pets, backgrounds). Close other tabs.

Captions

No text showingChrome/Edge only. Mic must be active. Internet required (Chrome sends to Google). Check Activity Log for errors. See Section 15.3.
Wrong languageFollows app language setting (Settings → Language).

micro:bit

Not found in pairingFlash firmware first. Power cycle micro:bit. Chrome/Edge only. No other app using BLE.
Disconnects oftenAuto-reconnect (3 attempts). Stay within 10m. Check battery.

Camera

Not in dropdownCheck USB. Different port. Refresh page. Close other apps using it. See Chapter 3.
Black screenAnother app has the camera. Close Zoom/Teams. Refresh.
Phone disconnectsKeep screen on. Same Wi-Fi. App in foreground. See Section 3.3.

General

Won't loadClear browser cache. Clear service worker.
Lost settingsCleared browser data = localStorage wiped. Use Export Session to backup.
Focus mode stuckEscape or click "✕ Exit Focus" button (top-right).
Piano no soundFixed in v0.7.201. AudioContext now auto-resumes. Refresh the app.
Can't find Fun tools⋯ More → scroll down to "Fun" group. Popup is scrollable.

Privacy

100% local. Nothing uploaded. No accounts. No analytics. No cookies. Zero network calls from JS (fonts self-hosted). Only exception: Live Captions use browser's built-in speech service (may send audio to Google/Microsoft). All data in localStorage — clearable anytime via Settings → Clear Cache.

24 Advanced Features Reference

24.1 Timelapse Recording

In Settings → Recording: enable Timelapse and set multiplier (2×, 4×, 6×, 8×, 10×). The recording runs at reduced frame rate (30fps ÷ multiplier), resulting in a sped-up video. Perfect for long build sessions or soldering timelapses.

24.2 Time Goal & Auto-Stop

In Settings → Recording: set a target duration (minutes). A countdown chip appears on canvas. Optionally enable auto-stop — recording stops automatically when the goal is reached.

24.3 Screensaver

After 90 seconds of no user input (and not recording), an idle screensaver activates: a drifting emoji with a "press any key to wake up" hint. Any mouse/key/touch event dismisses it. Toggle in Settings.

24.4 Intro/Outro Cards

In Settings → Recording: enable cinematic cards. A 2.5s intro card (brand title + scene name + "Action!") plays at the start, and a 2s outro card (Merci! + badge summary) at the end. Both baked into the recording.

24.5 Onboarding Tour

On first launch, a 5-step guided tour highlights key areas: Sources, Canvas, Scenes, Recording, Settings. Runs once (gated by tc-tour-done in localStorage). Can be re-triggered from Help panel.

24.6 Session Export & Import

  • 📦 Export Session — Settings → Maintenance. Downloads a JSON file with all tc-* localStorage keys.
  • 📥 Import Settings — upload a previously exported JSON. Replaces all settings. Page reloads.

Use this to back up your setup, transfer settings to another computer, or share your configuration with colleagues.

24.7 Multi-Select Sources

Shift+click to add/remove sources from the selection. Multi-selected sources show a sky-blue dashed outline. Move all selected sources together by dragging one. Ctrl+A or "Select All" button to select all visible sources.

24.8 Snapshot Features

  • Snapshot Gallery Strip — pressing S downloads a PNG AND adds an 88×50 thumbnail to a scrollable strip below the stage. Click to re-download, hover for delete ✕.
  • Multiplier — in Settings: capture at 1×, 2×, or 4× canvas resolution (up to 7680×4320).
  • Annotation Mode — in Settings: when enabled, snapshots open in an annotation modal where you can draw/add text before saving.

24.9 Scene Auto-Save

When you drag/resize a source or change its properties (skin, border, etc.), the active custom scene is automatically re-saved. Toggle in Settings. No need to manually click "Save Layout" after every change.

24.10 Scene Auto-Advance

In Settings: automatically advance to the next scene after N seconds. A countdown pill appears at top-right of the canvas. Useful for timed presentations.

24.11 Layout Undo/Redo History

Ctrl+Z undoes the last layout change (source position/size/visibility). Ctrl+Y redoes. 10-step history. Each drag/resize end, scene switch, and source add/remove creates a history entry.

24.12 Retry 30s (Soft Rewind)

Ctrl+Z during recording flags the last 30 seconds as a retry region. A chapter marker is added with an orange tint. The trim editor can later auto-cut these retry regions.
